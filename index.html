I am a PhD researcher in Geospatial Data Science at Ariel University, Israel, with a strong foundation in computer science and over 14 years of academic teaching and research leadership across Ethiopian universities. My work sits at the intersection of machine learning, urban mobility, and spatial intelligence — building systems that are not only accurate but interpretable and actionable for decision-makers.

My research interests span time-series forecasting, predictive modelling, resource optimisation, applied AI for real-world systems, healthcare analytics, emergency service demand modelling, and explainable geospatial intelligence. I am particularly focused on the integration of multimodal spatial and temporal data for interpretable decision support systems in urban environments.
